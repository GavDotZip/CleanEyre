The Three Rs: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Irish households produce approximately 1.5 million tonnes of waste each year. Nearly half of this rubbish is general waste, which is collected from black bins and goes to landfill. Only a quarter of this waste is collected from our green and brown bins to be recycled or composted.

Reducing Your Waste

Avoiding the production of waste is the greatest method to reduce it. Use items again whenever possible. For instance, use a reusable bottle and fill it up at home or at a water fountain to avoid purchasing plastic water bottles. Bring your own reusable shopping bags if you're going shopping.
Here are some tips to reduce your waste:

  1. Shop smarter by buying frozen goods which will keep longer
  2. Use shopping apps such as ToGoodToGo which help battle food wastage
  3. Purchase a BPA free plastic bottle or a metal bottle to avoid reusing and wasting plastic
  4. Instead of binning clothes, bring them to a charity shop
  5. Don't leave lights and other appliances on unnecessarily
These tips will go a long way to reducing waste, and saving the environment, as well as your pocket!

Reusing Your Waste

You can "reuse" materials in their original form instead of throwing them away, or pass those materials on to others who could use them too! Remember, one man's trash is another man's treasure!
Here are some tips to reuse your waste:

  1. Use reusable goods such as coffee cups and water bottles
  2. Make wise packaging decisions around how much you buy
  3. Instead of binning leftovers, freeze them, or keep them for the next day
  4. Take up upcycling as a hobby to reuse depreciated household items
  5. Take broken items to a local hardware store, they may have some use for them
These tips will go a long way to help you reuse everyday items, and saving the environment, as well as helping others out!
